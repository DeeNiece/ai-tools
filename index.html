What's New in v2.1

v2.1 is a complete rewrite of the signal detection engine. Every signal now reads the horizontal histogram bins directly — not just price bar position. The result is fewer, higher-quality signals that are directly grounded in institutional order flow.

ADDEDEmpty Bin Rule for Edge Exhaustion — EDGE EXH fires only when at least 1 empty bin exists at the profile boundary. Empty bin = zero institutional activity at that price = genuine exhaustion. Validated against 9 real chart examples across 7 symbols.
ADDEDPOC Integrity Check for VA TRAP — VA TRAP BUY now requires POC bin to show buyer dominance. VA TRAP SELL requires seller dominance at POC. Eliminates false VA TRAP signals when POC is broken.

ADDEDLVN Check for Rejection — Rejection signals at thin/empty boundaries are suppressed as stop hunts. Only fires when real volume exists at the boundary.

CHANGEDDiscord embed colors — Fired signals: green (BUY) or red (SELL) embed. Blocked signals: yellow embed. Clear visual distinction at a glance in Discord.
ADDEDAI Bias Analysis button — ▶ AI Analysis button next to BIAS SUMMARY header. Sends profile data to AI provider on click. Shows ⏳ loading state while waiting. Displays 4-sentence bias below rule-based summary. Never triggers automatically — only on manual click.

Section 01

What is VolumeEdge Pro?

VolumeEdge Pro is a Fixed Range Volume Profile (FRVP)Plots a horizontal histogram of traded volume over a fixed number of bars. Each horizontal bar = one price bucket (bin), width = volume at that price. Shows where institutional activity was concentrated. indicator for cTrader. It draws horizontal volume histograms on your chart and fires signals based on what the histogram bins show — not just where price is.

Core FRVP Levels

POC — POINT OF CONTROL

Highest volume price level

Most traded price bin. Acts as a magnet. Shown as a yellow solid line.

VAH — VALUE AREA HIGH

Upper value boundary

Price above VAH = bullish breakout zone. Shown as a green dotted line.

VAL — VALUE AREA LOW

Lower value boundary

Price below VAL = bearish breakdown zone. Shown as a red dotted line.

VA — VALUE AREA

Institutional activity zone

Contains your configured % of total volume (default 40%). Where most activity occurred.

Signal Types

POC TOUCH · MEAN REVERSION

Price returns to POC

Best in ranging marketsADX below 20. Price oscillating without clear directional trend. (ADX < 20). Bin confirmation: POC zone must show dominant side with sufficient ratio.

VA TRAP · MEAN REVERSION

Price re-enters value area

Trapped breakout traders reversing. Bin confirmation: POC must show correct directional dominance to confirm the level is holding.

EDGE EXHAUSTION · MOMENTUM

Stalls at profile boundary

Best in trending marketsADX above 25. Price moving directionally with institutional backing. (ADX > 25). v2.1: fires only when empty bins exist at boundary.

REJECTION · MOMENTUM

Wick + volume spike reversal

Wick into key level with volume spike, close pulls back. v2.1: LVN check added — thin boundary = stop hunt = suppressed.

Section 14 · New in v2.1

Bin-Based Signal Engine

★
Core principle shift in v2.1

Signals no longer fire based on price bar conditions alone. The horizontal histogram BINSEach horizontal bar in the FRVP histogram = one price bucket. Width = volume traded at that price. The bins are drawn from historical data — they don't change bar by bar. They show the settled reality of where institutions traded. confirm or suppress every signal. Price bar position is only the trigger to start reading the bins. The bins are the judge.

Why bins, not price bars?

Price bars are noisy — a wickThe thin line above/below a candle body. Represents the high/low price briefly reached but not sustained. A wick touch at a level with zero bin volume = stop hunt or random volatility, not institutional activity. can touch a level with zero volume. The histogram bins show you the settled reality of where institutions actually traded. A bin with zero volume means nobody traded there — that IS the signal.

BinZoneAnalysis — what it measures

PropertyMeaning
IsLVNLow Volume NodeA price level where very little volume was traded. Price moves THROUGH LVNs easily with no resistance or support. If the boundary is an LVN, price can continue beyond it — not a reversal signal. — bin volume below LvnThreshold % of POC. Too thin to trust.
IsHVNHigh Volume NodeA price level where heavy two-sided institutional activity occurred. Both buyers and sellers traded heavily here. Acts as strong support/resistance — price tends to respect and react at HVNs. — heavy two-sided activity. Both sides > 30% of total AND total > 50% of POC.
BullDominantBuy volume > sell volume × 1.2. Buyers in control of this zone.
BearDominantSell volume > buy volume × 1.2. Sellers in control.
IsBalancedNeither dominant. No directional convictionWhen buy and sell volume are close to equal at a level, neither side controls it. Price may oscillate without a clear reaction. Signals at balanced zones are lower quality. — suppress signals.
ImbalanceRatioDominant side / weaker side. Must exceed requiredRatioThe minimum imbalance ratio for a signal to fire. Set by ADX regime and symbol. BTCUSD trending = 2.0 (need 2x dominance). EURUSD ranging = 1.1 (slight dominance sufficient). Higher ratio = stricter, fewer signals. for signal to fire.

ADX sets the required ratio — instantly, no bar counting

The required imbalance ratio adjusts based on current ADX value. No bar-by-bar comparison — just the current reading:

Section 15 · New in v2.1

Edge Exhaustion Logic

★
The Empty Bin Rule

EDGE EXH fires when at least 1 empty binA price bucket with TotalVols = 0. Means zero trades occurred at that price during the lookback window. Price briefly visited it but nobody was willing to trade there — instant rejection. The most objective exhaustion signal possible. exists at the profile boundary. An empty bin means price briefly visited that level with zero institutional activity — genuine exhaustion. This single rule, validated against 9 real chart examples, replaces all previous complex pattern logic.

What an empty bin means

18571 / 0 (IMB) — buyers pushed here
12801 / 2810 (IMB) — buyers dominant
[ empty bin ] ← nobody traded here
0 / 25998 (IMB) — seller wall
▼ EDGE EXH SELL fires — empty bin confirms buyer exhaustion at boundary

EDGE EXH SELL — fires when

At least 1 empty bin exists in the top boundary zoneThe outermost bins near the profile High. Scanned depth = BoundaryScanPct% of total bins. EURUSD: ~4 bins. BTCUSD: ~8 bins. If any of these are empty = price reached the High with zero institutional conviction. (near profile High). Price briefly touched that level with zero institutional interest = buyers had no conviction at the High.

EDGE EXH BUY — fires when

At least 1 empty bin exists in the bottom boundary zoneThe outermost bins near the profile Low. If any are empty = sellers pushed price to the Low but couldn't find anyone to sell to. The void below the Low means sellers had no conviction — price must reverse. (near profile Low). Price briefly touched that level with zero institutional interest = sellers had no conviction at the Low.

Validated across 9 real chart examples

EURUSD BUY
3 empty bins at Low → price UP ✓
USDCAD BUY
Near-empty bins at Low → price UP ✓
AUDJPY BUY
3 empty bins at Low → price UP ✓
AUDUSD BUY
1 empty bin at Low → price UP ✓
AUDUSD SELL
1 empty bin at High → price DOWN ✓
CADJPY SELL
1 empty bin at High → price DOWN ✓

Gray + empty bins → price DOWN ✓
BTCUSD BUY

XAGUSD BUY

⚠️
False signal protection

BTCUSD had heavy seller IMBs (0/44022, 0/17000) with zero empty bins at the Low — price continued DOWN. XAGUSD had a massive 0/113322 seller bin — no empty bins, price continued DOWN. Both correctly blocked. The empty bin rule protects you from mistaking heavy selling for exhaustion.

💡
Why not gray bins?

Gray bins (EXH tag, volume <15% of POC) show thinly traded levels — not zero. We considered adding gray bin logic but found it introduced ambiguity (how many? what threshold?). The empty bin rule is objective: volume is either 0 or it isn't. Keep it simple — add gray logic later only if you find consistent real examples.

POC Touch / VA TRAP / Rejection — v2.1 Updates

POC Touch — bin zone confirmation

Price touching the POCPoint of Control — the price bin with the highest traded volume in the lookback window. Acts as a magnet for price. The most important level in the FRVP. is not enough. The ±2 bins around the POC must show directional dominance:

ConditionResult
POC zone BullDominant + ratio ≥ requiredPOC BUY fires ✓
POC zone BearDominant + ratio ≥ requiredPOC SELL fires ✓
POC zone IsBalanced (50/50)Suppressed — no conviction at POC
POC zone IsLVN (weak node)Suppressed — POC has no volume weight

VA TRAP — POC integrity check

Before firing a VA TRAP signal, the indicator checks whether the POC is actually holdingPOC integrity = the POC bin shows more volume on the expected side. For VA TRAP BUY: POC bull volume > bear volume × 1.2. If POC is seller-dominant when you expect a buy reversal, the level is broken — price will continue down. in the expected direction:

SignalPOC must showIf POC shows opposite
VA TRAP BUYBuyers dominant at POC — POC holding as supportSuppressed — POC broken, price will continue down
VA TRAP SELLSellers dominant at POC — POC holding as resistanceSuppressed — POC broken, price will continue up
💡
Why this matters

The classic false VA TRAP: price drops below VAL, bounces back into VA, VA TRAP BUY fires — but POC shows sellers dominant (20059/46904 seller-biased). POC is broken. Price continues down. v2.1 suppresses this correctly.

Rejection — LVN stop hunt filter

Rejection signals at thin/empty boundaries are suppressed as stop huntsA stop hunt occurs when price briefly spikes beyond a key level to trigger stop-loss orders, then immediately reverses. Thin/empty bins at the boundary = very few orders there = easy for large players to push price through briefly. These are not genuine rejections.:

Boundary volumeResult
Boundary IsLVN (thin/empty)Suppressed — thin boundary = stop hunt, not genuine rejection
Boundary has real volumeExisting wick + volume spike logic applies → may fire

Section 18

Gate Performance Audit

The [B] tag in recent outcomes is your gate performance reportEvery blocked signal shows in the analysis panel with a [B] tag and the outcome it would have had. Over time, patterns in [B] WIN vs [B] LOSS tell you if your gates are helping or hurting your edge.. Three scenarios:

SCENARIO 1 — Mostly [B] WIN rows

Gates are too strict

Good signals being filtered. Action: lower MinConfluenceGate from 2 to 1, or reduce CooldownBars, or check session filter hours for this symbol.

SCENARIO 2 — Mostly [B] LOSS rows

Gates are working perfectly

Bad setups being caught before they hurt you. Do not change anything — the gates are your edge.

SCENARIO 3 — Mixed [B] results

Look at which gate is blocking

If most [B] WIN are blocked by cooldown but [B] LOSS are blocked by confluence — confluence gate is good but cooldown is too aggressive. Reduce CooldownBars. Keep confluence at 2.

💡
Decision Rule

If blocked LOSSes > blocked WINs in total pips → gates net-positive, keep them.
If blocked WINs > blocked LOSSes in total pips → gates net-negative, loosen them.

Section 23 · Concept

What are Bins?

BinsShort for histogram bins — equal-width price buckets that divide the profile's price range. Each bin records how much volume was traded within that price slice. are the fundamental building blocks of the Fixed Range Volume Profile. The name comes from statistics — a histogram groups data into equal-width buckets called bins.

How bins are created

When you set a lookback of 24 bars on H2, the indicator reads every bar in that window and finds the overall High and Low. That price range is then divided into equal slices — one slice per bin:

BTCUSD H2 — 50 bins — price range 76828 to 77134 (306 points)
Bin 50 — @77128 BuyVol:15526 SellVol:0 (IMB)
Bin 49 — @77122 [empty — nobody traded here]
Bin 48 — @77116 BuyVol:11601 SellVol:10204 (ABS)
Bin 25 — @76702 BuyVol:42990 SellVol:25789 (HVN) ← POC
Bin 3 — @74196 BuyVol:0 SellVol:37257 (IMB)
Bin 2 — @74190 [empty — nobody traded here]
Bin 1 — @74184 [empty — nobody traded here]

What each bin stores

FieldMeaning
BuyVolTotal buy-side volume traded within this price slice. A bar where Close ≥ Open contributes its volume here.
SellVolTotal sell-side volume traded within this price slice. A bar where Close < Open contributes here.
TotalVolsBuyVol + SellVol. Used for width of the histogram bar.

Why bin size matters

Bin size = (High − Low) ÷ number of bins. More bins = finer resolution but noisier. Fewer bins = cleaner but loses precision at key levels.

SymbolDefault binsWhy
EURUSD30Tight range, 30 bins gives ~3 pip resolution per bin
XAUUSD40Wider daily range, needs more bins for precision
BTCUSD50Massive range, 50 bins keeps resolution meaningful

The empty bin — the most powerful signal

An empty binTotalVols = 0. Price passed through this price level so quickly that zero volume was recorded. The market had zero interest in trading at this price. This is the strongest exhaustion signal — genuine price rejection. means TotalVols[b] == 0. Nobody traded at that price during the entire lookback window. Price visited that level briefly but the market had zero interest in transacting there — it was immediately rejected. This is the foundation of the EDGE EXH signal in v2.1.

💡
Think of it like an auction

Each price level is an auction. If nobody shows up to bid or offer at a price, that auction fails instantly. An empty bin = a failed auction = the market doesn't want to trade there = price must reverse.

Section 01

What is POC Magnet?

POC Magnet is based on one of the most reliable principles in volume analysis: price always returns to the Point of Control. The POCPoint of Control — highest-volume price in the profile. Where most institutional activity occurred. When price moves away, it creates an imbalance the market constantly works to resolve by returning. is where institutions built their positions. When price drifts far away, it creates an imbalance that resolves by returning.

Your job: identify when price is far from POC, determine which direction it needs to travel, and position yourself on that journey.

🧲
The magnet principle

A D1 POC sitting 40+ pips away is a magnet with enormous gravitational pull. The further price is from POC, the higher the probability it returns. D1 POC = strongest magnet. H2 POC = medium-term mid-target. M30 POC = entry guide.

Section 02

Why POC Always Pulls

REASON 01

Mean Reversion

Price oscillates around equilibrium. POC is the most recent equilibrium. Extended moves always self-correct. Statistical, not opinion.

REASON 02

Institutional Memory

Large players who built positions at POC defend that level. When price returns they add or take profits — both push price back toward POC.

REASON 03

Unfilled Orders

Naked POCsPrior session POCs price has never revisited. Unfilled institutional orders still waiting there. Every session without a return increases the pull — until it snaps back violently. from prior sessions = unfilled institutional orders. The longer price stays away, the stronger the eventual pull.
