How to Use Horror Story Studio

This tool generates a complete 8–10 minute YouTube script in 5 sections, plus thumbnail assets and YouTube metadata. Everything saves to your Google Sheet for tracking and reference.

Workflow: Generate script → Copy each section into HeyGen → Download avatar videos → Assemble in CapCut with B-roll from Pexels → Add Suno music → Upload to YouTube.

Step-by-Step Production Workflow

1
Generate the script — Enter your story idea, select niche and audience, click Generate. Takes 20–40 seconds.
2
Hook — Copy Hook script into HeyGen. Avatar full screen, 16:9. Generate talking photo video. This is your opening 30 seconds.
3
Setup, Escalation, Resolution — Copy each section into HeyGen separately. Download each avatar video. In CapCut, shrink avatar to bottom-right corner, fill main screen with B-roll footage from Pexels using the keywords shown.
4
Text overlays — In the Escalation section, add CapCut text overlays at the [TEXT:] cue points marked in the script. Bold, high-contrast font. 2–3 seconds duration each.
5
CTA — Copy CTA script into HeyGen. Avatar back to full screen. Keep energy warm and direct.
6
Suno music — Generate a background track in Suno using the mood description from each section. Layer it in CapCut at 15–20% volume under all narration.
7
Thumbnail — Generate background image in Gemini using the Gemini Image Prompt. Add the Thumbnail Text on top in CapCut or Canva. Check for AI text rendering errors.
8
Upload — Use the YouTube Title, Description and Tags directly from the tool. No editing needed. Change Status in Sheet to "Uploaded" after publishing.

Google Spreadsheet Column Reference

When you click Save to Sheet, the tool creates 3 tabs automatically — FinanceHorror, LegalHorror, and DramaHorror — one per niche. Each tab has the same column structure. The correct tab is selected automatically based on your niche selection when you generate.

ColumnWhat it contains & how to use it
Episode#Auto-generated timestamp number. Unique ID per script. Use this to track which video is which when you have many rows.
TimestampDate and time the script was generated. Useful for tracking your production pace and keeping episodes in chronological order.
NicheFinance, Legal, or Drama. Filter this column to see only scripts of one type when planning your upload schedule.
Story IdeaThe raw idea you entered. Keep this as your master story reference to avoid repeating the same story across future episodes.
YouTube TitleCopy this directly into YouTube when uploading. Already SEO-optimized and under 70 characters. Do not shorten it further.
Hook ScriptWord-for-word script for your HeyGen avatar — the opening 30 seconds. Paste directly into HeyGen text input. Avatar should be full screen for this section.
Setup ScriptScript for 0:30–2:00. Introduces the characters and situation. Avatar goes to corner. B-roll plays in background. Use B-Roll Setup column for footage keywords.
Escalation ScriptThe main story body (2:00–8:00). Longest section. Contains [B-ROLL:] and [TEXT:] inline cues. Follow these exactly in CapCut for maximum dramatic effect.
Resolution ScriptWhat happened in the end (8:00–9:00). Legal outcome, financial outcome, justice or injustice. Keep avatar in corner with B-roll.
CTA ScriptLesson and call to action (9:00–10:00). Avatar returns full screen. Do not cut this short — the subscribe ask at the end is important for channel growth.
B-Roll SetupComma-separated keywords for sourcing stock footage on Pexels or Pixabay for the Setup section. Search each keyword and pick the most dramatic clip.
B-Roll EscalationKeywords for the Escalation section B-roll. More keywords here because it is the longest section. Match the clip emotionally to the script moment it covers.
Thumbnail TextThe 4–7 word text overlay for your thumbnail. All caps. Add this in CapCut or Canva on top of your Gemini-generated background image.
Thumbnail Gemini PromptPaste this into Gemini Image Generate to create the thumbnail background. Do not include this text in the image — it is background only. Add text separately.
Avatar ExpressionSuggested expression for your HeyGen talking photo thumbnail screenshot. Screenshot your avatar mid-script at the right expression, crop, use as thumbnail base.
YouTube DescriptionFull video description. Paste directly into YouTube description field. Already includes hashtags. You can add your channel links at the bottom.
YouTube Tags15 comma-separated tags. Copy and paste into YouTube tags field during upload. Helps YouTube understand your content category.
Suno Hook MoodMusic mood description for the Hook section. Use this as your Suno prompt to generate the opening background music. Keep it tense and dramatic.
Suno Escalation MoodMusic mood for the Escalation section. Usually more intense than the hook mood. Generate a separate Suno track for this section for variety.
StatusDefaults to Draft. Manually update this to: In Production (when you start editing), Ready (when video is done), Uploaded (after publishing to YouTube). Use this to track your pipeline.

B-Roll Tips

Search B-roll keywords on Pexels.com or Pixabay.com — both are completely free with commercial use license. Download the highest resolution available. In CapCut, layer B-roll below the avatar video. Set avatar video to picture-in-picture, resize to about 25% of screen, anchor to bottom-right corner.

HeyGen Tips

For best results use a talking photo — upload a royalty-free Filipino woman photo from Pexels. Search "Filipino woman professional" or "Asian woman teacher." Photo must be front-facing, clear face, neutral background. Use 16:9 aspect ratio for YouTube. Generate each section separately — Hook, Setup, Escalation, Resolution, CTA as 5 separate HeyGen videos.

Suno Tips

In Suno, use Instrumental mode only — no lyrics. Use the Suno mood descriptions from the sheet as your style prompt. Generate 2–3 variations and pick the best. In CapCut, set music track volume to 15–20% so it never competes with the avatar narration. Fade out at the end of the CTA section.
